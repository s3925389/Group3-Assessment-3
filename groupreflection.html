Nicole Attard - S3924819

Working with this group of people has truly been such an enjoyable experience. Juggling personal and family commitments, to be able to maintain focus and make great efforts to ensure effective communication in order to create a product has been a great achievement. Initially starting out having basic, limited knowledge in the process involved in developing a chatbot, my time working with this team on this project has been a valuable experience in not only the practical aspect, but also the planning and documentary aspects.


One area where we could have improved for this project would have been at the start. I feel we could have more clearly set out the roles for everyone especially in the development process. However, this would have been particularly difficult given the limited knowledge we as a group had in relation to creating a chatbot but also the fact that given the scope of the project, the issues that we had in relation to working in real time collaboratively on a shared file where members would accidentally overwrite the work of others would have possibly arisen and complicated the process.


Overall, I feel we were able to meet our main objectives, having discussed and analysed feedback given following our first project together as a team. We effectively limited the scope and achieved basically what we hoped to: a chatbot that responds appropriately to text-based inputs from a user. However, despite our best attempts, we were unable to host our chatbot on a server which would have enabled users without the project files to interact with the chatbot. Given the skills and ability of the entire team that has been demonstrated throughout this time, I believe that with more time, this is an achievable outcome.

Group Reflection


After receiving our results for the first group assessment, the team reflected on the results and the feedback. Our meetings on the this was productive, and we all wanted to improve in the next assessment. But overall, we believe our work we completed was a success, but we had room to improve in different areas. All up, it was a great effort.


The team have been meeting twice a week to discuss the process and progress of the next two group assessments. As a group, we all decided to proceed with the Chatbot as our project idea. The idea of the Chatbot we chose was Cris’s idea and as a group we all agreed with the idea. The group found this as a challenge in all ways. Majority of the team members didn’t know much about the processes of creating the Chatbot or the background, so this would be a learning curve for us.


Once our idea was in place, our focus turned to the first submission of a draft for the presentation. The team discussed what we should put in and the script for it. The layout was completed, and the focus was quickly shifted to the main Assessment.


Laying out the sections on a word document and discussing the areas what we should do, the group agreed on which sections and timeframe we should have completed and be put in the draft. Identifying the main section of the project were the marks lay, the group came up with a theme for the Chatbot. The Knights Templar was born! He was our face for the project idea. He will be a funny but serious Knight that would help the user around websites that it was assigned for. The members of our team agreed on to choose a couple of sections to work on for the project.


Going back to the presentation, our work would be displayed on a PowerPoint format with music and voice overs. The work of our members to put it altogether was outstanding. The creativity of the members was fascinating, and we all thank each other for the effort everyone put in.


In the last week before the project was due, our meetings became important to see where the project was at and what areas required to be completed. The Chatbot was running and talking back, which was a good thing. We had the final product ready. Going over the completed assessment, one last meeting was performed. The team looked over the website to check any mistakes or issues. Once we all agreed everything looked fine, Submitting was the next step.


The past 2 months working as a group, strangers coming together for one common goal showed us how teams work in the real world of IT. We all became good friends throughout our studies and hoping we can keep in touch in the real world. We all wish each other the best success in our career paths in the future.
